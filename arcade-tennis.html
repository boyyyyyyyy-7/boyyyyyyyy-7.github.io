üéæ How to Play & Controls

The controls in Arcade Tennis are designed to be fluid and responsive, ensuring that your on-screen player moves exactly when you want them to. Most players find that using the arrow keys or WASD offers the best precision for positioning. The spacebar or dedicated action keys are used to swing your racket, with the timing of your press determining the power and angle of your shot.

Serving is your first opportunity to take control of the point. A well-placed serve can force a weak return, setting you up for a devastating volley at the net. During the rally, pay close attention to your player's position relative to the ball; hitting the ball too early or too late will lead to inaccurate shots that your opponent can easily exploit. Mastery comes from learning how to redirect the ball's momentum to keep your opponent constantly on the move.

üí° Pro Tips & Strategy

To truly dominate the court, you need to think two steps ahead. One of the most effective strategies is "cross-court cornering"‚Äîrepeatedly hitting the ball to one corner of the baseline to pull your opponent wide, then suddenly smashing it to the opposite side once they're out of position. This makes it almost impossible for even the toughest AI to recover in time.

Don't just stay glued to the baseline! Sneaking up to the net (the "serve and volley" technique) can be a high-risk-high-reward move. By shortening the distance, you give your opponent less time to react, but you also leave yourself vulnerable to a well-placed lob. Learn to read your opponent's animations; often, they will telegraph where they are going to hit the ball a fraction of a second before they do it, giving you the edge you need to win the set.
