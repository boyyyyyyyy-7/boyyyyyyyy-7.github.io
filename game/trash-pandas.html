About Trash Pandas: Junkyard Run

🦝 An Original Build-and-Run Game

Trash Pandas: Junkyard Run is an exclusive, in-house physics game you won't find anywhere else. It plays in two phases: first you build a ramshackle machine out of junkyard parts, then you hit GO and watch the physics play out as your contraption lurches across the level. Your one job is to get the precious cookie jar safely to the raccoon den.

It's a love letter to classic contraption-builder games, but every level, part, raccoon and junkyard is built from scratch right here. No two builds run the same way, and half the fun is watching your over-engineered cart faceplant in glorious slow motion.

🔧 How to Play — Build, Then Run

In the build phase, drag parts from the bin onto the darkened grid. Parts snap onto neighbours, so you grow your machine outward from the cookie jar. Place wheels to roll, a fan or balloon to fly, springs to bounce, and frames to hold it all together. Drag a part back to the bin to remove it. When you're happy, press GO.

In the run phase the simulation takes over — you can't steer, but you click parts on the machine to fire them at the right moment: detonate TNT, pop a balloon, trigger a soda-bottle blast. Press R to jump back to the workshop and tweak your design, or Esc to pause. Your build is your strategy.

⚙️ Parts & Physics

Every part behaves like the real thing. Wooden frames are light and flexible but snap under hard hits; iron frames weigh more but hold a sturdier machine together. Wheels roll, fans and balloons give you lift, springs bounce, and TNT and soda bottles deliver the chaos. Parts flex, break off on big impacts, and tumble away as debris.

The cookie jar is your cargo — it's fragile, so a bad landing can shatter it and end the run. But here's the trick: the jar wins by reaching the den by any means. Even if your machine explodes and the jar is thrown loose across the finish line, that still counts. Build smart, or build a glorious accident.

💡 Tips for Beginners

Start simple — a jar on two wheels with one fan often clears the early junkyards. Add weight low and keep the jar padded so it survives the bumps. If a run fails, hit R and change one thing at a time so you learn what actually helped.

Chasing all three stars? You'll need to finish with the cookie jar healthy, beat the time, and grab the star box that's tucked off the easy path — usually that means a second, riskier build. Experiment freely; retries are unlimited.
